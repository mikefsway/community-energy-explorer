About this map

This tool overlays four things for England:

  • Need — the English Indices of Deprivation at neighbourhood (LSOA) level. More deprived places stand to gain most from lower bills and local energy income.
  • Existing community energy — organisations assembled from the Community Energy England member directory, energy-named registered societies on the FCA Mutuals Register, and energy-focused charities, geocoded to their registered location. Shown separately from project sites (installed community schemes from the community-maintained CEE map extract, 2024 vintage).
  • Energy Redress funded projects — grantees of the Ofgem Energy Industry Voluntary Redress Scheme (administered by Energy Saving Trust), a useful marker of organisations that have already delivered funded energy work. Points sit at the stated project town where known, otherwise the grantee's registered office.
  • Energy knowledge bases — universities and companies leading UKRI-funded energy research (Gateway to Research, sized by project count), plus a curated set of sector anchors: DNO head offices, the system operator, the regulator, catapults and community-energy intermediaries.
  • Community fabric — proxies for local civic capacity: registered charities per resident and community venues (village halls, community centres) from OpenStreetMap.
  • Grid capacity — primary-substation headroom published by the Distribution Network Operators, shown as red/amber/green.

The Need × readiness view crosses deprivation (need) with community fabric and existing organisations (readiness): dark red areas are high-need but thin on civic infrastructure (support must be built, not just funded); dark teal areas have strong fabric and lower need; the deep purple corner — high need and strong fabric — is where community energy could take root fastest and matter most.

Caveats: organisation locations are registered offices, which may differ from where projects operate. Community fabric proxies are partial — they miss informal networks. Grid headroom is indicative; always check the DNO's own capacity map. Sources and method: see the repository.
